vel › compare 2JZ-GTE and LS3 for forced induction potential

The 2JZ-GTE runs a twin-turbo inline-6 with cast iron block — handles boost pressure up to 800hp+ on stock internals. The LS3 is a 6.2L V8 with aluminum block, naturally aspirated by design but responds well to supercharger kits. For high boost builds the 2JZ has the edge on bottom end durability. The LS3 wins on displacement and peak power ceiling with proper forced induction setup.


vel › what block material handles sustained boost better and why

Cast iron. Higher density means better thermal mass — it absorbs and distributes heat more evenly under sustained boost. Aluminum expands faster under heat which can cause seal failures at high boost over time. Cast iron blocks like the 2JZ and RB26 are why those engines have a reputation for surviving big power numbers on stock blocks.
